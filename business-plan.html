Executive Summary

The Epstein Files Research Platform is a proprietary AI-powered document analysis tool built on the 3.5 million pages released by the U.S. Department of Justice on January 30, 2026 under the Epstein Files Transparency Act. While the source documents are public, they are functionally inaccessible — a 3.5 million page corpus that no individual can meaningfully search without purpose-built tooling.

This platform provides semantic search, entity extraction, network relationship mapping, and AI-powered query across the entire corpus. The value proposition is simple: we searched 3.5 million pages so researchers, journalists, and the public do not have to.

Timing Advantage The DOJ released the bulk of the Epstein files on January 30, 2026 — 28 days ago. Congressional hearings, ongoing redaction controversies, and active civil litigation keep this story in national headlines weekly. The relevance window for peak monetization is approximately 6–18 months from today.

Technical Architecture

  • Backend: .NET 9 Web API with PostgreSQL 17 (with pgvector for embeddings)
  • Frontend: React / TypeScript with real-time pipeline dashboard
  • AI Layer: Vector embeddings for semantic search; RAG pipeline for Q&A
  • Auth: JWT-based with role-tiered access (freemium → basic → premium → media/enterprise)
  • Ingestion: Python extraction pipeline with OCR, chunking, and embedding workers
Key Differentiator The platform is fully operational. The document ingestion, OCR, vector embedding, and semantic search pipeline is built and running. This is a go-to-market and packaging exercise, not a build exercise. Time-to-launch is measured in days, not months.

Go-to-Market Strategy

The strategy is deliberately sequenced. Phase 1 prioritizes quality over volume — landing credible media partners before any public announcement. Their subsequent reporting becomes the marketing engine for Phase 2, the public rollout.

Phase 1 Media & Professional Access
Duration: Launch (Mar 13) through ~June 2026

Who:
  • Investigative journalists (Substack, independent)
  • Digital news outlets with investigative desks
  • Podcast producers covering the story
  • Authors / book researchers
  • Law firms with active civil cases

How:
  • Direct outreach — email and LinkedIn
  • 30-day free trials for journalists
  • Founding Partner rate: $500/mo (locked for 12 months)
  • No public announcement until 3+ partners signed

Goal: 8–12 paying media/pro accounts, 2–3 published stories that cite the platform.
Phase 2 Public Launch
Duration: ~Month 4 onward (July 2026+)

Who:
  • General public / true crime enthusiasts
  • Political researchers and activists
  • Students, academics
  • Anyone following the Epstein story

How:
  • Public landing page + free signup tier
  • Launch on X/Twitter with specific findings as hooks
  • "As featured in [Outlet]" social proof
  • Beehiiv/Substack email list as nurture → paid funnel
  • Weekly newsletter: AI-extracted findings from the corpus

Goal: 20,000+ free list, 2,000+ paid subscribers within 90 days of launch.

Content Marketing Engine

The most cost-effective acquisition channel is publishing specific, shareable findings directly from the corpus. Each "finding post" on X/Twitter with a concrete, verifiable claim (e.g., "Document #EFTA-2307 names [individual] in 14 separate flight logs — here's the AI-extracted summary") drives free signups organically. Target: 2–3 posts per week during peak window.

Risk Analysis

Risk Severity Mitigation
Story loses news momentum — interest fades before Phase 2 generates sufficient subscribers High Launch Phase 2 no later than Month 4. Build email list from Day 1. Diversify to general true crime angle beyond Epstein.
DOJ publishes its own searchable interface for the document corpus Medium Unlikely given government IT timelines, but hedge by emphasizing AI analysis layer — not just document hosting — as the differentiator.
Legal challenge — claims of defamation via AI-extracted connections Medium Engage lawyer before launch. All AI outputs cite source documents. Add clear disclaimer: analysis is AI-generated from public records.
Subscription fatigue — market saturation makes subscriber acquisition harder Medium Free email list first. Convert with weekly finding newsletters. Offer annual plan discount (2 months free).
Infrastructure cost overrun at high query volume Low Rate-limit free tier. Cache common AI queries. Current architecture is efficient; costs grow slowly relative to revenue.
Payment processor refusal (Stripe content policy) Low Platform serves public DOJ records — clearly legal. However, have fallback processor (Paddle or Lemon Squeezy) ready.
